Poster & Web Page Design by Saurabh Karthikeya  •  About this design

About this Design


Poster:

Tools Used:
- Affinity Publisher
- GIMP

Background: Elliptical Gradient with Noise texture
Foreground (Tiles): Linear Gradient with Noise texture

Font Faces:
Title: Mrs Saint Delafield (Google Fonts)
Subtitle: Monoton (Google Fonts)
Text: Graphik

Inspired by Figma Community Riffs and Apple product spotlights

The poster was meant to be a diversion from the standard "university poster" aesthetic, and leans more towards a editorial/magazine spread style document.
In my opinion, the Noise texture in the background and on the tiles contributes a lot to that than meets the eye.
Everyting is intentionally uniformly sized, uniformly spaced, and uniformly aligned.
Its not minimal for the sake of being minimal. It isn't. Rather, it just keeps what's necessary on the print, and lets tis web page handle additional information that would've otherwise cluttered the poster.
I used a bar code to link to this page instead of a QR code, even tho its far more unconventional and inconvenient, just because it simply looks better.

Web Page:

Tools Used:
- HTML
- CSS

Background: Elliptical Gradient with Noise texture
Foreground (Tiles): Linear Gradient with Noise texture

Font Faces:
Title: Mrs Saint Delafield (Google Fonts)
Subtitle: Monoton (Google Fonts)
Text: Quicksand (Google Fonts) (due to Graphik licensing limitations - macOS gave it to me for free and spoilt me for every other sans font)

Just inspired by the poster as I thought the visual language should carry on to the web page as well, as it is merely an extension of the actual poster.
Honestly vibe coded, but it gets the job done (this popup was fully written by me tho).
Had to keep in mind how it'd look on phones as well.

Like with most of my work, both the poster and web page designs were driven by typography. It all starts by finding a good font to carry the content.
Why purple background? Well it's my fav color. And noise is the best thing that ever happened to colors. So purple + noise. I thought that would be too boring, so I introduced an orange/peach gradient.
